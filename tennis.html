  1. Roger Federer

    Widely regarded as one of the greatest tennis players of all time, Roger Federer won 20 Grand Slam singles titles. Renowned for his elegant playing style, versatility, and sportsmanship, Federer held the world No. 1 spot for a record 310 weeks. His rivalries and grace on and off court made him a global ambassador for tennis.

  2. Rafael Nadal

    Known as the "King of Clay," Rafael Nadal has dominated Roland Garros with a record 14 French Open titles and 22 Grand Slam singles trophies overall. Celebrated for his relentless intensity, physicality, and humility, Nadal is admired for his fighting spirit and legendary rivalries with Federer and Djokovic.

  3. Novak Djokovic

    Novak Djokovic has rewritten the record books with the most Grand Slam singles titles in men’s history (24 as of 2025). Famous for his mental toughness, flexibility, and complete game, Djokovic has held all four Grand Slams at once and finished year-end No. 1 a record eight times.

  4. Pete Sampras

    v

    Pete Sampras dominated tennis in the 1990s with his powerful serve-and-volley game, capturing 14 Grand Slam singles titles. He held the world No. 1 ranking for a then-record 286 weeks and is revered for his calm demeanor and clutch performances, especially at Wimbledon.

  5. Rod Laver

    Rod Laver is the only player in tennis history to achieve the Grand Slam twice—winning all four major titles in a calendar year (1962 and 1969). A true pioneer, Laver’s dominance spanned both the amateur and Open Eras, and his legacy to this day remains as a benchmark for greatness.
